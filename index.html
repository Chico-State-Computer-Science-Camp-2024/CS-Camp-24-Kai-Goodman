Mantis Shrimp

Mantis Shrimp are small, but mighty marine crustaceans that have long, colorful bodies. They are often found in tropical and subtropical waters of the Indian and Pacific Oceans between Eastern Africa and Hawaii. What really makes them special is the punching-power of their front limbs.

  • Punches can reach 60 MPH, with a force that is comparable to a bullet
  • They grow up to 18 inches long and can be blue, green, red, and orange
  • They have very complex eyes that can see a huge variety of wavelengths
  • They are highly resistant to infections
  • Some can even produce light from their bodies
